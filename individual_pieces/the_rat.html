the rat

My hands have been sweating bullets like a criminal in an interrogation room.
In a way it’s quite symbolic.
I, the criminal, clearly didn’t prepare enough.
How on earth did they find the body?
Someone must have ratted.
As I survey the room, anxiety building, anger peaking, sorrow slowly fills my heart.
I have been betrayed by a comrade, someone I’ve fought alongside the past 3 years.
My eyes fixate on the window in front of me.
I’ve given up on finding the rat.
Ignorance is bliss & my heart could not handle the pain anyways.
Through the blinds I can see the gentle wind caressing the newly grown leaves.
Like a newborn experiencing all the joys of the earth, I wonder if they feel euphoric as the wind cools their surface.
What am I doing?
Why have I personified a leaf?
I’m still staring though.
The edges past the blinds are poking out.
The test time is almost done, the world is showing its face to me again.
I realize I’ve probably failed this test.
At least the 10 minutes spent writing this was enjoyable.
I’ve maintained my focus on the window, its oddly captivating.
There’s something so familiar about it.
It’s a reflection. That’s why.
The betrayal is destroying me.
No comrade could ever possibly disappoint me this much.
As I see myself in the reflection, I now know who the rat is.
It’s me.
