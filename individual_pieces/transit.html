transit

The last few days have been spent in hyper orbit. As a result, I have become dependent on various forms of transportation, most of it public, to keep the circus in business. Having now been fully immersed into the ecosystem of the following services I have taken it upon myself to conduct a comprehensive comparison of the TTC & GO transit ventilation systems.

Having spent most of my semi-professional career in this area, I suppose this is the one time you can trust what you’re reading is only 40% bullshit. Despite it’s overpriced, aggressive & obnoxiously noisy ventilation system circulating air at an excessive CFM rate; the GO’s air filtration system fulfills the primary goal of any respectable air filtration system. Minimize hideous odours exiting the perspiring pores of its innumerable, inconsiderate, mostly overweight, patrons.

For the sake of transparency, I should disclose that I have been accused of being a smelly fellow by many of my dearest friends; however, I have & will continue to vehemently deny all of these fallacious claims. This has nothing to do with my ability to objectively analyze ventilation systems, I said it purely to defend my honour.

When it comes to ventilation, the TTC applies the risk to reward principle in a very unique way. Maximize risk while minimizing reward so as to create a hostile, uncomfortable, yet incredibly comedic environment which lends itself well to all the heathens, provocateurs, provocatrixes & rough-jacks of the city. You will more often than not finish your TTC excursion with one or more of the following scents: AXE cologne coming from a local fanook in jorts, the unique smell of sweat specific only to the underside of a BBW’s breasts as her cardiovascular system panics due to her overexertion from overeating, or a fresh takeout bag from ‘The Owl of Minerva’

Leaving the TTC craving a deep cleanse is the most likely outcome after any trip; however, the occasional whiff of fresh Baseema exuding from the bottom-less purse of a Sudanese grandma, that she’s bringing on the 85 to her grandchildren at Meadowvale & Sheppard, will certainly restore your belief in human purity, momentarily at least.

Written by Joseph Pongonthara on a park bench littered with pigeon shit. (Victoria St & Lombard St - 23’/05/16)
